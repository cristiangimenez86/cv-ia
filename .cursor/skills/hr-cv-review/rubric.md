# HR CV Review Rubric

Use a 0-100 overall score derived from weighted dimensions.

## Scoring Dimensions

1. **Positioning and Clarity (20%)**
   - Clear headline and role alignment
   - Concise professional summary
   - Seniority signaled correctly

2. **Impact and Achievements (25%)**
   - Outcomes over task lists
   - Quantified impact where possible
   - Business relevance is explicit

3. **Role Relevance and Keywords (20%)**
   - Matches target role expectations
   - Includes role-specific vocabulary
   - Avoids keyword stuffing

4. **ATS Compatibility (20%)**
   - Parse-friendly structure and chronology
   - Unambiguous job titles
   - Stable section naming and date formatting

5. **Writing Quality and Credibility (15%)**
   - Strong, direct phrasing
   - Consistent tense and grammar
   - Claims are believable and evidence-based

## Decision Bands

- **85-100: Strong** — Ready for high-confidence applications.
- **70-84: Competitive** — Good baseline; improve key gaps before priority applications.
- **55-69: Needs Work** — Multiple issues reduce interview conversion probability.
- **0-54: High Risk** — Significant rewrite needed before sending broadly.

## Severity Model for Findings

- **High**: likely causes ATS filtering or recruiter rejection.
- **Medium**: reduces competitiveness but not immediate rejection.
- **Low**: polish improvements with incremental gains.

## Minimum Output Quality Rules

- Every high-severity issue must include a concrete fix.
- At least one ATS-related and one recruiter-related risk must be reported when present.
- Rewrite suggestions must preserve user-provided facts (no invented numbers).
